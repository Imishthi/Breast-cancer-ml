Types of Breast Cancer

DCIS
Ductal Carcinoma In Situ (DCIS): Non-invasive, Stage 0 breast cancer that has not spread into other tissue or lymph nodes. Highly treatable.
IDC
Invasive Ductal Carcinoma (IDC): Begins in the milk ducts and spreads into surrounding breast tissue or areas of the body.
ILC
Invasive Lobular Carcinoma (ILC): Starts in the lobules and invades surrounding tissue or other parts of the body.
TNBC
Triple Negative Breast Cancer: Aggressive form that lacks estrogen, progesterone, and HER2 receptors, limiting treatment options.
IBC
Inflammatory Breast Cancer (IBC): A rare, fast-growing cancer affecting the breast skin and often causing a distinct rash or redness.
MBC
Metastatic Breast Cancer (MBC): Stage 4 cancer that has spread to distant areas like the bones, liver, lungs, or brain.
