Early Detection

Understanding the Signs and Symptoms

Recognizing the early signs of breast cancer is crucial for timely diagnosis and treatment. Awareness empowers individuals to take proactive steps towards their health. Knowing what to look for and when to seek help can make all the difference. Educating yourself and others is a powerful step in reducing risk and improving outcomes.

Common Physical Changes

  • New lump or mass in the breast or underarm
  • Swelling or thickening of part of the breast
  • Irritation or dimpling of breast skin
  • Redness or flaky skin in the nipple area
  • Pulling in of the nipple or pain in the nipple area
  • Nipple discharge other than breast milk, including blood
  • Any change in the size or shape of the breast
  • Pain in any area of the breast
  • Unexplained rash around the nipple
  • Sudden visible veins on the breast
  • Itchy or scaly skin on the breast or nipple
  • Swelling or lumps around the collarbone or armpit area
  • Inversion (turning inward) of the nipple
  • Skin on the breast appearing puckered or like an orange peel (peau d’orange)

When to See a Doctor

If you observe any of the above signs, consult a healthcare professional promptly. While these symptoms can also occur with non-cancerous conditions, it's important to investigate them early. Early diagnosis improves the chances of successful treatment and provides peace of mind. Regular checkups, self-examinations, and open communication with your doctor are key to staying ahead.
